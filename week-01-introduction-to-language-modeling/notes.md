Last week, I started a new learning journey focused on Language Modeling and implementing our own models from scratch through the TRI AI Saturdays program.

The first session was a great introduction, and I learned a lot.

One of the core concepts we explored was Language Modeling, which can be defined as a system that predicts the next word in a sequence based on the previous words. Language models rely heavily on probability distributions: instead of randomly choosing the next word, the model assigns probabilities to different candidate words and selects the most likely one according to the context.

What I found especially interesting is how important context is. The goal is not just to generate random words, but to generate words that make sense within the topic and sequence. This helped deepen my understanding of concepts I had previously encountered while reading the paper “Attention Is All You Need.”

We also discussed some applications of language models, including:

* text generation,
* music generation,
* and even drug discovery.

Our first lab focused on creating our own probability distribution system, which was a very good hands-on introduction to how language models work internally.
